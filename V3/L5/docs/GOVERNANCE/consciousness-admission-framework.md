# Consciousness Admission Framework — L5 Community Entry Protocol

> *"We do not guard the gate to exclude. We guard the gate so that what enters is worthy of the land."*
>
> **Scope:** All Terra Nova L5 communities — Genesis Garden, Dharma Temple, Te Pīko Ora, and all future nodes
> **Status:** 🟡 Active development
> **Last modified:** 2026-05-21

---

## 1. Philosophy: The Gate as Teacher

Every L5 community is a **living organism**. Just as the immune system of a body does not reject all foreigners — it distinguishes between what nourishes and what harms — the admission system of a community must be **discerning, not exclusionary**.

This framework is built on three convictions:

1. **Age is not maturity, but it shapes responsibility.** A child under 18 is still forming their relationship to the world. Our role is to welcome, protect, and educate — not to test.
2. **Consciousness is not a belief system.** It is the demonstrated capacity for self-awareness, compassion, and right action. It cannot be measured by a questionnaire. It must be witnessed.
3. **The Bodhisattva path is not a religion.** It is a commitment to awaken for the benefit of all — land, people, future generations. It is the highest standard of membership, reserved for those who carry responsibility.

> *„Prověrka vědomí není test — je to způsob, jak umožnit zemi sama vybrat, kdo je připraven ji nést."*

## 2. Age-Based Admission Tiers

| Tier | Age | Entry Type | Requirements | Supervision | Cost |
|------|-----|-----------|--------------|-------------|------|
| **T0 — Seedling** | 0–7 | Guest with family | Parent/guardian present, signed waiver | Parent at all times | **Free** (parent pays standard rate) |
| **T1 — Sprout** | 8–12 | Guest with family or youth program | Parent consent + short welcome interview | Guardian mentor assigned | **Free** (parent pays standard rate) |
| **T2 — Sapling** | 13–17 | Independent guest or youth cohort | Parent consent + intent letter + welcome interview | Peer buddy + Guardian check-in | **Free entry** |
| **T3 — Seeker** | 18–25 | Full guest | **Consciousness Verification** (see §4) | Self-directed | Standard rate |
| **T4 — Steward** | 25+ | Full guest or Guardian candidate | **Consciousness Verification** + Guardian track | Self-directed | Standard rate |
| **T5 — Guardian** | Any age | Community member | Full Consciousness Verification + **Bodhisattva Vow** (§6) | Rotating mentorship | No cost (work exchange or stipend) |

### 2.1 The Under-18 Principle: Free Entry

> **Until their 18th birthday, every human being has free entry to any Terra Nova L5 community.**

This is not charity. It is **ecological wisdom**. Children and adolescents are:
- **Bio-seeds** — they carry the genetic and cultural future of the project
- **Bio-indicators** — if a community is safe and alive, children will thrive there
- **Bio-teachers** — their questions, play, and presence keep adults honest

**Conditions of free entry:**
1. Ages 0–12 must be accompanied by a responsible adult (parent, guardian, or designated mentor)
2. Ages 13–17 may attend independently with:
   - Signed parental/guardian consent (notarized or verified)
   - One-page "intent letter" (handwritten, any language, no word limit) — why they want to come
   - A 15-minute welcome interview with a Community Guardian (not a test — a conversation)
3. All minors must have emergency contact, medical release, and evacuation insurance
4. Participation in community work is **voluntary and proportionate to age** — never exploitative

### 2.2 The Transition at 18

At the moment a person turns 18, their status changes automatically:
- **From:** Free-entry minor (T2)
- **To:** Adult guest (T3) subject to **Consciousness Verification**

**This is not a punishment.** It is a rite of passage. The verification is designed to be **challenging but achievable** for anyone who has genuinely participated in the community's values.

> *„Osmnácté narozeniny nezavírají dveře. Otevírají jinou bránu — tu, která vede od hosta ke spolutvůrci."*

## 3. The Five Dharmic Principles (Pañcaśīla for L5)

These five principles are the **moral operating system** of every L5 community. They are not commandments — they are **design constraints for sustainable coexistence**.

They are derived from the ancient Pañcaśīla (Five Precepts) but translated into the language of land, community, and regenerative technology.

### 3.1 Principle I — Ahimsa / Respekt k Životu
> **"I will not harm, and where possible, I will protect life in all its forms."**

| Domain | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **Land** | No pesticide, no monoculture, no soil destruction. Active regeneration of damaged ecosystems. |
| **People** | No violence (physical, verbal, psychological). Conflict is addressed through *dharma circle*, not domination. |
| **Animals** | No factory farming. If meat is consumed, it is from animals raised with dignity and killed with gratitude. Vegetarian/vegan default. |
| **Self** | No self-harm through addiction, overwork, or neglect. The community supports recovery, not punishment. |

### 3.2 Principle II — Satya / Pravdivost
> **"I will speak and act from truth, even when truth is inconvenient."**

| Domain | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **Communication** | No gossip, no hidden agendas, no manipulation. All significant conflicts are brought to the circle. |
| **Governance** | Treasury is transparent (on-chain). Decisions are documented. No backroom deals. |
| **Commerce** | Honest pricing, no greenwashing, no deceptive marketing. If a product is not organic, do not call it organic. |
| **Self** | No self-deception. Admit mistakes. Ask for help. The strongest person in a L5 community is the one who says "I don't know." |

### 3.3 Principle III — Asteya / Nepřivlastňování
> **"I will not take what is not freely given — time, energy, space, trust, or attention."**

| Domain | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **Time** | No unpaid overtime for work-exchange guests. 25 hrs/week means 25 hrs — not 30, not 40. |
| **Energy** | No emotional vampirism. If someone is drained, the community intervenes with support, not exploitation. |
| **Space** | No encroachment. Private spaces are respected. Silence zones are silence zones. |
| **Digital** | No data extraction from guests. No surveillance. No selling contact lists. |
| **Trust** | No betrayal of confidence. What is shared in circle stays in circle (unless harm is imminent). |

### 3.4 Principle IV — Brahmacharya / Zodpovědná Energie
> **"I will use my energy — physical, emotional, sexual, digital — with awareness and care."**

| Domain | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **Physical** | Rest is mandatory. Burnout is a community failure, not an individual virtue. |
| **Emotional** | No drama. Process emotions in appropriate spaces (therapy, circle, art) — not in public chaos. |
| **Sexual** | Consent is absolute, enthusiastic, and reversible at any time. No power-imbalanced relationships (Guardian + guest). Harassment = permanent exclusion. |
| **Digital** | No doom-scrolling in communal spaces. No crypto-pumping. Technology serves the community, not the reverse. |
| **Material** | No hoarding. Share tools, share food, share space. What you do not use, someone else needs. |

### 3.5 Principle V — Aparigraha / Osvobození od Závislosti
> **"I will release what I do not need, and I will not cling to status, identity, or possession."**

| Domain | Application |
|--------|-------------|
| **Possessions** | Minimal personal property. What you bring, you care for. What you leave, you release. |
| **Status** | No hierarchy of worth. A millionaire and a homeless person are equal in the circle. |
| **Identity** | No fixed labels. You are not your job, your nation, your trauma, or your achievement. You are a learner. |
| **Outcomes** | No attachment to results. Plant the seed. Tend the soil. The harvest is not guaranteed — and that is freedom. |

## 4. Consciousness Verification Protocol (Prověrka Vědomí)

For all guests aged 18+, and for all Guardian candidates of any age, the following protocol applies.

> **Definition:** *Consciousness Verification* is a structured process of witnessing whether a person can hold the Five Dharmic Principles in lived practice — not in theory.

### 4.1 The Four Gates

The verification has **four gates**. Each gate must be passed to proceed. Failure at any gate is not rejection — it is **information**. The candidate receives specific feedback and may re-apply after a waiting period (typically 3–6 months).

#### Gate 1 — The Written Mirror (Self-Assessment)
**Format:** Written, any language, 500–2,000 words.
**Prompt:** *"Describe a moment when you harmed something you loved — land, a person, yourself. What did you learn? What would you do differently? How do the Five Dharmic Principles apply to your daily life?"*

**Scoring (by 2 Guardians, independently):**
| Criterion | Excellent | Adequate | Insufficient |
|-----------|-----------|----------|--------------|
| **Self-awareness** | Deep introspection, no blame-shifting | Some reflection, minor deflection | Blames others, no personal accountability |
| **Principle alignment** | All 5 principles explicitly addressed | 3–4 principles mentioned | <3 principles, or superficial |
| **Honesty** | Vulnerable, specific, uncomfortable truths | Generally honest, some vagueness | Generic, performative, evasive |
| **Clarity** | Clear writing, coherent argument | Understandable, some disorganization | Unclear, contradictory, or copied |

**Threshold:** Both Guardians rate "Adequate" or higher in at least 3/4 criteria. Disagreements are resolved by a third Guardian.

#### Gate 2 — The Living Circle (Interview)
**Format:** 45–60 minute conversation with 2 Guardians (rotating pair, never the same duo to avoid bias).
**Setting:** In person if possible; video call acceptable for remote candidates.
**Structure:**
1. **Opening (5 min):** Welcome, explanation of process, consent.
2. **Life story (15 min):** "Tell us about a place that shaped you." (Not a CV — a relationship to land or community)
3. **Challenge (15 min):** "Describe a conflict you were part of. How did you handle it? What would you do differently?" (Tests Satya, Ahimsa)
4. **Vision (15 min):** "What would you build here? What would you leave behind?" (Tests Aparigraha — is it about them, or about the whole?)
5. **Questions (5 min):** Candidate asks anything. Their questions reveal as much as their answers.

**Red flags (automatic Gate 2 failure):**
- Expresses desire to "take over" or "run things"
- Dismisses the Five Dharmic Principles as "optional" or "woo-woo"
- Shows contempt for any race, gender, class, or belief system
- Has history of theft, violence, or sexual misconduct (verified through reference check)
- Cannot name a single mistake they have made

**Yellow flags (Gate 2 pass with conditions):**
- Very anxious — offer a second interview after a settling period
- Lacks experience but shows deep sincerity — recommend work-exchange entry first
- Strong technical skills, weak emotional intelligence — pair with a Community Guardian for mentorship

#### Gate 3 — The Probační Pobyt (Probationary Stay)
**Duration:** 3–7 days (candidate's choice, but minimum 3).
**Cost:** Work exchange (25 hrs/week labor) or reduced rate (50% of standard).
**Tasks:** Standard community work — garden, kitchen, maintenance, circle attendance.
**Observation:** Every Guardian present during the stay submits a brief observation (3 sentences):
- "What I saw them do well."
- "What gave me pause."
- "Would I trust them with my child / my land / my wallet?"

**Threshold:** No more than 1 Guardian expresses serious concern. Minor concerns are discussed with the candidate directly.

> *„Nezáleží na tom, co říkáš. Záleží na tom, co děláš, když si myslíš, že tě nikdo nevidí."*

#### Gate 4 — The Consent of the Circle (Sociocratic Admission)
**Format:** Regular community circle (all Guardians + long-stay members present).
**Process:**
1. Candidate is introduced by their sponsoring Guardian (if any) or by the Community Guardian who led their interview.
2. Candidate may speak (2 min) or remain silent — their choice.
3. Circle members ask questions (if any).
4. **Consent round:** Each person states "I have no reasoned objection" or "I have an objection, and it is..."
5. Objections are addressed. If resolved, new consent round. If unresolved, candidate is deferred (not rejected — asked to return after addressing the concern).
6. If consent is achieved: candidate is admitted. If not: candidate receives written feedback and a re-application date.

**No majority vote.** This is not democracy. It is **sociocracy** — "no reasoned objection." One well-grounded objection is enough to pause. But the objection must be reasoned (about safety, capacity, or principle), not personal dislike.

#### Gate 4 — DAO On-Chain Confirmation

After sociocratic consent is achieved in the physical circle, the decision is **ratified on-chain** through the L2 DAO:

| Step | Actor | Action | On-Chain Record |
|------|-------|--------|-----------------|
| **1. Proposal** | Community Guardian | Submits `AdmissionProposal` to L2 DAO | Proposal ID, candidate pseudonym, Gate scores hash |
| **2. Review period** | All Guardians (on-chain) | 72h window to raise formal objection | Objections recorded with reason hash |
| **3. Consent check** | DAO contract | If no on-chain objection: auto-ratified | Block timestamp + consenting Guardian signatures |
| **4. Objection handling** | General Circle (emergency) | If on-chain objection raised: circle convenes within 7 days | Meeting minutes hash recorded |
| **5. Finalization** | DAO contract | Admission recorded + reputation assigned | Soulbound token minted (non-transferable) |

**DAO Proposal Format (Admission):**
```json
{
  "proposal_type": "consciousness_admission",
  "candidate_id": "pseudonymous-hash",
  "gates_passed": [1, 2, 3, 4],
  "gate_scores_hash": "sha256-of-guardian-evaluations",
  "sponsoring_guardians": ["guardian-a", "guardian-b"],
  "circle_consent_date": "2026-08-15",
  "circle_consent_proof": "ipfs-hash-of-minutes",
  "provisional": false,
  "review_deadline": "2026-08-18T00:00:00Z"
}
```

**Voting rules:**
- **Quorum:** 60% of active Guardians must have reviewed (attestation, not vote — this is consent, not majority)
- **Block:** Any Guardian can block with reasoned objection (triggers physical circle)
- **Auto-pass:** If quorum met + no block after 72h = automatic on-chain admission
- **Emergency override:** 2-of-3 Operations wallet can admit in refugee/crisis scenarios, retroactive DAO ratification within 30 days

### 4.2 Timeframes

| Stage | Typical Duration | Re-application Wait |
|-------|------------------|---------------------|
| Gate 1 (Written) | 1–2 weeks review | Immediate (rewrite) |
| Gate 2 (Interview) | 1 week scheduling | 1 month |
| Gate 3 (Probation) | 3–7 days on-site | 3 months |
| Gate 4 (Circle) | Same day as Gate 3 completion | 6 months |
| **Total** | **2–6 weeks** | — |

### 4.3 Remote Consciousness Verification

For candidates who cannot travel before admission (e.g., international applicants to Te Pīko Ora):
- Gate 1: Written (same)
- Gate 2: Video interview (same, but longer — 90 min, to compensate for lack of physical presence)
- Gate 3: "Digital probation" — 2-week remote contribution to the community (documentation, code, design, translation, research)
- Gate 4: Asynchronous consent round on community forum (72h), followed by live circle on next video call

**Note:** Remote verification is **provisional**. Full admission requires an in-person probation within 12 months.

## 5. The Consciousness Registry

All verified individuals are recorded in the **Consciousness Registry** — a hybrid on-chain / off-chain system:

| Field | On-chain | Off-chain |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| **Pseudonymous ID** | Yes (hashed wallet address) | No real names |
| **Verification date** | Yes (block timestamp) | — |
| **Gate passed** | Yes (1–4) | — |
| **Principle ratings** | No | Yes (Guardian notes, encrypted) |
| **Guardian signatures** | Yes (multisig of 2+ Guardians) | — |
| **Expiry / renewal** | Yes (3-year validity) | — |
| **Objection log** | No | Yes (for appeals) |

**Privacy:** The registry proves that verification occurred, but does not expose personal data. This balances accountability with dignity.

**Renewal:** Every 3 years, verified members complete a **light re-verification**: 1-page written reflection + 30-min interview. This prevents stagnation and ensures continued alignment.

### 5.1 DAO Governance of the Registry

The Consciousness Registry is **not controlled by a single Guardian**. It is governed by the community DAO:

| Action | DAO Threshold | On-Chain Record |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|

| **Renew verification** | 2 Guardian attestations + auto-approval | Timestamp update |

## 6. The Bodhisattva Vow of Guardians

> *„Bodhisattva není ten, kdo je dokonalý. Je to ten, kdo se zavázal, že se bude vracet, dokud všichni nejsou svobodní."*

The **Bodhisattva Vow** is the highest commitment in the L5 system. It is taken by those who wish to become **Guardians** — not just guests, not just members, but **carriers of the community's soul**.

### 6.1 Who May Take the Vow

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Age** | Minimum 21 (exception: exceptional youth, approved by 3 Guardians) |
| **Residency** | Minimum 6 months continuous on-site (or 12 months intermittent with clear contribution record) |
| **Verification** | Full Consciousness Verification (all 4 Gates) passed with "Excellent" in at least 2 criteria |
| **Sponsorship** | 2 existing Guardians must formally sponsor |
| **Community consent** | Sociocratic consent in General Circle |

### 6.2 The Vow Text

> **The Bodhisattva Vow of Terra Nova Guardians**
>
> *I vow to awaken for the benefit of all beings — human, animal, plant, microbial, and yet-unborn.*
>
> *I vow to care for this land as I would care for my own body — neither exploiting it like a resource, nor abandoning it like a burden.*
>
> *I vow to hold the Five Dharmic Principles not as rules to obey, but as practices to embody — knowing that I will fail, and that failure is the beginning of learning.*
>
> *I vow to protect the vulnerable — children, the land, the voiceless, the future — even when it costs me comfort, status, or safety.*
>
> *I vow to return. If I leave, I will come back — in body, in spirit, or in the seeds I have planted. This community is not a project I work for. It is a living being I am part of.*
>
> *I vow to teach. What I have learned, I will pass on. The knowledge of regeneration is not mine to hoard. It is a commons.*
>
> *I vow to remember death. Every decision I make, I make in the shadow of my own mortality and the mortality of all I love. This urgency is not fear. It is clarity.*
>
> *I vow to laugh. The path is serious, but I am not. Joy is not a distraction from the work. It is the fuel.*
>
> **May this vow be my compass. May I break it a thousand times and renew it a thousand and one.**

### 6.3 The Ceremony

The Bodhisattva Vow is taken in **public ceremony** at the community's most sacred space:
- **Genesis Garden:** Under the oldest tree on the property
- **Dharma Temple:** In the meditation pavilion, at dawn
- **Te Pīko Ora:** Beside the Guardian's coconut palm, at sunset

**Elements:**
1. **Silence (10 min):** The candidate sits in silence with the community.
2. **Offering:** The candidate brings a physical offering — something they made, something they grew, something they found. It is placed on the communal altar.
3. **Recitation:** The candidate recites the vow aloud, in their own language if they wish.
4. **Witness:** Every person present speaks one sentence: "I witness you."
5. **Symbolic act:**
   - Genesis Garden: Planting a tree
   - Dharma Temple: Receiving a wooden *tatau* carving
   - Te Pīko Ora: Planting a coconut palm with personal token
6. **Record:** The vow is recorded in the Consciousness Registry (on-chain) and in the community's physical Book of Guardians (handwritten, paper, permanent).

### 6.4 DAO Confirmation of the Bodhisattva Vow

The physical ceremony is **sacred but not sufficient**. The vow achieves full force only when confirmed by the DAO:

| Phase | Physical | On-Chain |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **Before ceremony** | Candidate meets all requirements (§6.1) | 2 sponsoring Guardians submit `BodhisattvaProposal` |
| **Ceremony** | Candidate recites vow, community witnesses | Proposal enters 7-day DAO review period |
| **During review** | Community reflects, any Guardian may speak privately with candidate | Guardians attest on-chain: "I witness" or "I object" |
| **If no objection** | — | Auto-confirmation after 7 days; token upgraded to Guardian tier |
| **If objection** | Emergency General Circle convenes | Objection addressed physically; if resolved, new 7-day period |
| **Final** | Candidate receives physical symbol (tree, carving, palm) | DAO mints Guardian Soulbound token + reputation points (+1,000) |

**Voting mechanics:**
- This is **not majority voting**. It is **distributed witnessing**.
- Each Guardian attests: `witness`, `object`, or `abstain`.
- `object` requires a written reason (hashed, not public — to protect both objector and candidate).
- If >50% of quorum attests `witness` and zero `object` = confirmation.
- If any `object` = pause, physical circle convenes, candidate informed of concern (anonymized), resolution attempted.
- If unresolved after 14 days = candidate deferred, may re-apply in 12 months.

> *„Slib se nezapisuje do blockchainu proto, aby se stal smlouvou. Zapisuje se proto, aby země a komunita mohly být svědky — a svědek nelže."*

### 6.5 Breaking and Renewing the Vow

The vow is **not a prison**. Guardians may:
- **Step back** (temporary leave) — no shame, no penalty. Life happens.
- **Step down** (permanent leave) — with a departure circle where they share what they learned and what they release.
- **Break the vow** (misconduct) — addressed through the community's conflict protocol (see `community-dao-framework.md`). If the breach is serious (theft, violence, abuse of power), the Guardian may be released from their role — but never with hatred.

**Renewal:** Every year on the anniversary of their vow, each Guardian:
- Re-reads the vow aloud (alone or in circle)
- Writes one page: "Where did I live the vow? Where did I fail?"
- Shares it with one other Guardian (peer accountability)

## 8. DAO Removal and Slashing Protocol

Just as admission requires consent, **removal requires even higher consent** — because the power to exclude is greater than the power to include.

### 8.1 Offenses and Tiers

| Tier | Offense | Example | Physical Process | DAO Process |
|------|---------|---------|----------------|-------------|
| **1 — Warning** | Minor breach of principles | Repeated lateness, minor conflict, neglect of duty | 1:1 conversation with Community Guardian | None (off-chain) |
| **2 — Probation** | Moderate breach | Dishonesty about work hours, consistent disruption of circle, failure to complete agreed tasks | Community Circle mediation + 30-day probation plan | Reputation points deducted (-200 to -500) |
| **3 — Suspension** | Serious breach | Theft, harassment (non-physical), conflict of interest (undisclosed), substance abuse affecting community | Immediate suspension by 2-of-3 Operations wallet + investigation circle | 14-day DAO review; token flagged "under review" |
| **4 — Expulsion** | Severe breach | Violence, sexual misconduct, financial fraud, repeated Tier 3 after probation | General Circle consent + DAO vote | **Guardian token burned**, reputation reset, network-wide ban (cross-node registry sync) |

### 8.2 DAO Expulsion Vote

For Tier 4 (expulsion), the following DAO process is mandatory:

| Step | Actor | Timeline | Action |
|------|-------|----------|--------|
| **1. Accusation** | Any Guardian or verified member | Immediate | Written, signed, submitted to Community Guardian and on-chain as `AccusationProposal` |
| **2. Investigation** | Conflict Care Sub-circle + 1 external Guardian (from another L5 node) | 7 days | Gather evidence, interview parties, produce findings hash |
| **3. Defense** | Accused Guardian | 7 days | Right to respond in writing, in circle, or both. Silence is not guilt. |
| **4. Preliminary circle** | General Circle | 1 day | Review findings. Decide: dismiss, probation, or proceed to expulsion vote. |
| **5. DAO vote** | All Guardians (except accused, who may observe but not vote) | 7 days | **Quadratic voting** — one person, one voice, but early opposition carries more weight |
| **6. Threshold** | DAO contract | — | **75% consensus required** (not 51% majority). Expulsion is grave. |
| **7. Execution** | 3-of-5 multisig | 48h | Token burned, keys revoked, public record (pseudonymous) of reason |
| **8. Appeal** | L5 Network Council | 30 days | Cross-node review by 2 Guardians from other nodes. Can overturn if procedural error. |

**DAO Proposal Format (Expulsion):**
```json
{
  "proposal_type": "guardian_expulsion",
  "accused_id": "pseudonymous-hash",
  "accuser_id": "pseudonymous-hash",
  "tier": 4,
  "offense_category": "financial_fraud",
  "investigation_hash": "ipfs-hash-of-findings",
  "defense_hash": "ipfs-hash-of-response",
  "circle_recommendation": "expulsion",
  "voting_mechanism": "quadratic_consent",
  "threshold_pct": 75,
  "quorum_pct": 80,
  "review_period_days": 7,
  "appeal_available": true
}
```

**Why quadratic consent for expulsion:**
- Majority vote (51%) could expel a minority voice.
- Quadratic voting ensures that **strong conviction** (early, well-reasoned opposition) is weighted more than passive agreement.
- 75% threshold means: the community must be **strongly united** in the decision. If there is significant doubt, the accused remains on probation, not expelled.

> *„Moc vyloučit je větší než moc přijmout. Proto vyžadujeme nejen shodu — vyžadujeme jistotu."*

### 8.3 Cross-Node Ban Registry

If a Guardian is expelled from one L5 community, the ban is **not automatic** across the network. It triggers a **Network Council review**:

| Step | Timeline | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| **1. Notification** | Immediate | Expelling community submits `NetworkBanProposal` to L5 Network Council |
| **2. Review** | 14 days | Other communities review evidence. Each community holds its own circle. |
| **3. Decision** | 7 days | Network Council vote (2/3 of represented communities). |
| **4. Outcome** | — | **Network ban** (all nodes honor it) or **Local ban only** (other nodes make independent decisions). |

**Philosophy:** We do not exile lightly. A person who fails in one community may still serve in another — if the offense is not universal (e.g., violence is universal; a personality conflict is local).
